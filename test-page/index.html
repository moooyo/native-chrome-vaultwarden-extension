1 Login (single step) waiting

Focus the username or password field — the MiYu match panel opens to the right automatically. Pick an item to fill both fields.

2 Two-step login (username first) waiting

A username-only step (no password in scope) with a submit button. Focus the field — the panel offers a username to fill for the first step.

3 Two-factor code (standalone step) waiting

A lone autocomplete="one-time-code" field. Focus it — MiYu shows a live TOTP panel for the top matching login. Requires a vault login whose URI matches localhost and that carries a TOTP secret.

4 Registration (password generator) waiting

Focus the Create password field (a new-password field) — the inline generator panel offers a strong password and can save the new login. The confirm field is ignored on purpose.

5 Credit card waiting

Card fields tagged with cc-* autocomplete tokens. A 密屿 trigger appears under a field — click it and choose a card. Also works via right-click → 密屿, or Ctrl+Shift+F on a focused field.

6 Identity & address waiting

Name + address fields tagged with identity autocomplete tokens. Same triggers as the card: click the 密屿 icon, right-click → 密屿, or Ctrl+Shift+F.

7 Hint-only fallback (no autocomplete) waiting

Same card + identity data but classified only by name/id/placeholder text — exercises the regex fallback classifier when a site omits autocomplete tokens.

8 Passkey — register & authenticate ready

This is the new section. Order matters: Register first (stores a passkey in the unlocked MiYu vault, attached to a login you pick), then Authenticate. A result panel labelled MiYu means the extension handled the ceremony (duck-typed credential, not a real PublicKeyCredential); Native means it fell through to the browser/OS authenticator. Prerequisites: open the popup and unlock the vault, and use http://localhost (an IP rpId is rejected).
